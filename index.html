To Gagosian Gallery.folder
TO:GG.pdf

Dear Gagosian Gallery,

The days I mail myself to strangers are still the days I believe in art. I fold color into envelopes and send them to no one certain. Sometimes it arrives as heartbeat, Sometimes it comes back as silence.

I keep the soft things we send The sketches that failed, The words I almost said, The pieces of light I carried in my pockets. Every crease is an address, Every smudge a memory of movement. Hope travels even when no one stamps it.

The things we send are never just things. They are traces, they are people, they are the space between us. We send them because we want to be seen, And to see is to remember we exist.

...

It spreads. it reaches. it travels. It drifts between hands like wind in a hallway, Soft, unseen, unstoppable. It builds movements from whispers, Collapses walls made of noise and doubt. It opens new eras and buries old seasons. It calls out the world, and when no one listens, It hums beneath the floorboards still alive.

It hides. It shines. It bleeds. It heals. It is a language that forgets how to translate itself. It is medicine that tastes like memory, A mirror that remembers your name. It is both bureaucracy and rebellion, A diamond and the dirt that keeps it warm.

But it is also a body. It conceives us before we name ourselves. It holds what we break, Forgives what we leave undone. It feeds on our feeling And returns it changed quieter, truer. It learns. it breathes. It outlasts us. Love wears its patience here. Strength disguises itself as softness.

It takes its time. It takes time to be made. It takes time to be seen. It waits for hands brave enough to hold it, For eyes willing to linger, For hearts that listen without wanting to own.

It is taken for granted and still, it forgives. It forgives the rushing, The glancing, The forgetting. It forgives the world for not looking long enough, And waits, always, to be found again.

It is the bureaucracy of emotion The system that keeps the heart from collapsing. It is a child and an inheritance, A pulse and an afterthought, Beautiful, defiant, endlessly forgiving.

It is not mine to keep. It is what i send when language fails me, What remains when i am gone. It is the letter i keep rewriting, And the gallery keeps receiving.

Sincerely,
Cara Kwon
